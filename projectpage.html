UX & Visual Interaction at Code for America

PrimerPeso is an application that helps small businesses connect with government incentives.

PrimerPeso is an application I designed and developed with two teammates during my Code for America Fellowship year. We worked in partnership with the government of Puerto Rico to research how we could use technology to improve economic development.

This project presented distinct challenges- both from working in a Spanish-speaking country as well as promoting great design in the civic and governmental space.

From many hours of user interviews of government employees and community members in Puerto Rico, we realized that the government had many incentives available to foster small businesses, but most business owners didnâ€™t know about them. We wanted our application to bridge this knowledge gap.

In developing PrimerPeso we conducted over 80 hours of user interviews in Puerto Rico and spent several weeks combing this research and defining our user and MVP.

As the designer, I was responsible for the entire look and feel of our product (from the application itself to how we communicated our progress to the outside world) and the overall user experience.
