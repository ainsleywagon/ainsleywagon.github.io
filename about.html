I have always loved color and putting things in the right place. My childhood was spent obsessing over the perfect pairing of pink & green and putting Skittles in rainbow order. Since first meeting Adobe Illustrator in 2008, I’ve been making vector graphics and replicating these colors with CSS on the internet.

I studied Architecture in college and had the chance to explore product design, play with arduinos, and give a lecture about haptic installations. I wanted to design brighter, iterate faster, and have more control over the final product so I gravitated toward User Experience but have never lost my love of the physical designed world.

With particular experience working on small teams, I love shaping the visual direction of the design as well as the details of its implementation. I was most recently a 2014 Code for America fellow working with Puerto Rico and spent my year designing an application that (beautifully) matches small business owners with available government incentives. I am currently contracting and exploring the vast design landscape of San Francisco.
